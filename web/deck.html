02
The problem

Concrete is 8% of global CO₂ — and mix R&D is locked in a few corporate labs.

Most producers can't optimize their mix. And no plant will send its recipes to a third-party AI.
So the intelligence has to run on the plant's own hardware — private by construction.
03
What it is

A plant-private AI, self-hosted on an AMD GPU.

Floor Copilot 01

Answers the plant's own floor knowledge, cites the source, and refuses when it doesn't know.

Mix Committee 02

Four AI specialists debate a recipe change against a real strength model, and return a verdict.

Facts live in a swappable knowledge base; the fine-tuned model brings the reasoning and the voice.
04

The strength model learned slag's early-strength penalty from real data. QC blocks it on safety, over savings.

Where this goes

Every plant that can't afford a research lab — gets one it can run itself.

Small precast plants, builders, and producers in underserved regions: a private, affordable mix optimizer that keeps their recipes in the building and cuts cement.
Honest boundary: trained on wet-cast lab data; for dry-cast pavers we predict directions, not certified mixes. Load-bearing changes go to a qualified engineer.
